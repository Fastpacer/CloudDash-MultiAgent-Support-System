# Refund Policy

## Overview
Clouddash offers refunds for unused services under specific conditions. This document outlines when refunds are available, how to request them, and our refund process.

## Refund Eligibility

### Refunds Available

**Unused Subscription Time**
- Refund prorated for unused portion of subscription
- Example: Monthly plan, cancelled on day 20 of 30 → 10-day refund
- Calculated from cancellation date forward
- Requires request within 30 days

**Billing Errors**
- Duplicate charges
- Incorrect amount charged
- Unauthorized transactions
- Billing system errors

**Failed Service**
- Service unavailability >48 consecutive hours
- Critical functionality non-functional
- Data loss due to system error
- Only after support unable to resolve

**Overpayment**
- Extra payment by mistake
- Double-charged same invoice
- Paid for higher tier than intended
- Must request within 60 days

### Refunds NOT Available

**Services Rendered**
- Usage-based services already delivered
- Completed reporting period
- Add-on services used
- Custom development already performed

**Downgrades**
- Downgrading to lower plan tier
- Cost difference is future credit (not refund)
- Non-refundable fees may apply

**Free Trial to Paid**
- Trial period already used
- Features already accessed
- No refund for features tested
- Can cancel without paying

**Commitment Contracts**
- Annual plans partially used
- Enterprise custom terms
- Multi-year commitments
- Cancellation fees may apply

**User Error**
- Mistakes in selecting plan
- Entering payment info incorrectly
- Integrations not working (user configuration)
- Changing mind about platform

**Chargeback/Fraud Claims**
- Chargeback filed then reversed
- Fraud claim then found invalid
- Becomes non-refundable after dispute resolution

## Refund Amounts

### Refund Calculation Examples

**Example 1: Monthly Subscription Cancellation**
```
Plan: Professional ($100/month)
Cancellation date: Day 20 of 30
Days remaining: 10
Daily rate: $100 ÷ 30 = $3.33/day
Refund amount: $3.33 × 10 = $33.33
```

**Example 2: Annual Subscription Cancelled**
```
Plan: Professional ($1,000/year, annual rate)
Purchased: January 1
Cancelled: March 15 (day 74 of 365)
Days used: 74
Days remaining: 291
Daily rate: $1,000 ÷ 365 = $2.74/day
Refund amount: $2.74 × 291 = $797.34
Less 5% annual discount: $797.34 × 0.95 = $757.47
```

**Example 3: Billing Error - Duplicate Charge**
```
Correct charge: $100
Duplicate charge received: $100
Refund due: $100 (full amount)
```

**Example 4: Add-on Service**
```
Premium support add-on: $50/month
Used 10 days of 30-day month
Percentage used: 10/30 = 33%
Cost of usage: $50 × 0.33 = $16.50
Refund amount: $50 - $16.50 = $33.50
```

## Refund Request Process

### Step 1: Gather Information
Before requesting refund, collect:
- Invoice number
- Transaction date
- Amount charged
- Specific reason for request
- Any supporting documentation
- Communication history with support

### Step 2: Submit Refund Request

**Online:**
1. Log into Clouddash account
2. Go to **Settings** → **Billing** → **Invoices**
3. Find relevant invoice
4. Click **Request Refund**
5. Fill out request form:
   - Reason for refund
   - Description of issue
   - Desired resolution
6. Submit

**Email:**
1. Send email to **billing@clouddash.io**
2. Subject: "Refund Request - [Your Company Name]"
3. Include:
   - Invoice number(s)
   - Account email
   - Transaction date(s)
   - Refund amount requested
   - Detailed explanation
   - Any supporting files/screenshots

**Support Ticket:**
1. Go to **Settings** → **Support**
2. Click **Create Ticket**
3. Category: **Billing**
4. Subject: "Refund Request"
5. Provide same details as email

### Step 3: Request Review
- Submitted request reaches billing team
- Acknowledged within 24 hours
- May request additional information
- Review typically completes within 5 business days

### Step 4: Refund Decision
Request can result in:
- **Approved**: Refund processed
- **Partial**: Portion of amount refunded
- **Denied**: Request declined with explanation
- **Pending**: Requires further investigation

### Step 5: Refund Processing
Once approved:
- Refund processed to original payment method
- Timeline depends on payment method:
  - Credit/Debit card: 3-5 business days
  - Bank transfer: 5-7 business days
  - PayPal: 1-3 business days
- Email confirmation sent

## Refund Timeframes

### Request Timeline

**Best Results:**
- Request within 30 days of charge
- Highest approval rate
- Faster processing

**Acceptable:**
- Request within 60 days
- May require additional justification
- Approval not guaranteed

**Too Late:**
- Beyond 60 days
- Refund generally not available
- Exception: Service failure claims
- Must provide evidence

### Processing Timeline

After approval:
- **Immediate**: Transaction recorded
- **24-48 hours**: Refund initiated
- **3-7 business days**: Funds appear in account
- **Timeline varies** by payment processor

## Refund Methods

### Credit/Debit Card
- Refund credited to card used for purchase
- Appears as credit or adjustment on statement
- Takes 3-5 business days
- Must wait for bank to process

### Bank Transfer
- If paid by ACH or wire transfer
- Refund returned to same account
- Takes 5-7 business days
- Reference number provided

### PayPal
- Refund to PayPal account
- Can transfer to bank from there
- Takes 1-3 business days for PayPal
- Bank may add additional time

### Account Credit
- Issued as account credit instead of refund
- Can use for future charges
- Available for non-subscription charges only
- Request if prefer credit

### Check
- Mailed by US mail
- Only for refunds over $500
- Takes 10-14 days
- Must provide mailing address

## Partial Refunds

### When Partial Refunds Issued

**Example Scenarios:**
- Service issue partially resolved: 50% refund
- Misunderstanding about features: 25% credit instead of refund
- Late cancellation request: Prorated partial refund
- Billing error with multiple issues: Percentage adjustment

### Accepting Partial Refund

- Partial refund offered if:
  - Full refund not applicable
  - Some service value provided
  - Compromise resolution
- Accept or request full review
- If accepted, cannot re-request later

## Disputes and Chargebacks

### Chargeback Process

If customer files chargeback:
1. Payment processor notifies Clouddash
2. Account flagged, service may suspend
3. Clouddash provided opportunity to respond
4. Resolution through payment processor
5. Customer account may be terminated

### Chargeback Consequences

- Chargeback fees imposed by bank ($25-$100)
- Negative marks in payment processor history
- Account suspension or termination
- Future accounts may be declined
- Legal action possible in some cases

### Avoiding Chargebacks

- Contact support first if issue with charge
- Request refund through proper channels
- Charge reversal takes 3-5 days vs chargeback 30-90 days
- Chargeback should only be last resort

## Refund After Cancellation

### Data Access During Refund Period

**14-Day Grace Period:**
- After cancellation, data accessible for 14 days
- Download dashboards and reports
- Export raw data if needed
- Access removed after 14 days

### Refund During Grace Period

- Can request refund within 14 days of cancellation
- Still eligible for refund calculation
- Grace period allows time to decide
- After 14 days, no refund available

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I get a refund after using the service for a month?**
A: Refunds are for unused portion only. If cancelled day 20 of month, day 10-30 is refundable.

**Q: Will I get a refund if I was unhappy with the product?**
A: User satisfaction is not grounds for refund. However, we offer 30-day trial without payment.

**Q: How long does it take to receive my refund?**
A: After approval, 3-7 business days depending on payment method.

**Q: Can I get a refund on an annual plan if I cancel after 3 months?**
A: Yes, prorated for unused 9 months. However, annual discount typically stays applied to refund.

**Q: What if I have a billing error, can I dispute it?**
A: Yes, billing errors eligible for full refund. Submit with documentation.

## Prevention and Best Practices

- Review charges monthly on billing statement
- Keep payment method current
- Verify plan matches your needs before purchase
- Use free trial before committing to annual plan
- Contact support immediately if issues arise
- Understand refund policy before purchasing

## Appeals Process

If refund request denied:
1. Response email explains reason
2. Can request supervisory review
3. Provide additional information or context
4. Second review completed within 5 days
5. Final decision communicated
6. Can escalate further if extreme circumstances
