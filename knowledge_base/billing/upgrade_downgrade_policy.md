# Upgrade and Downgrade Policy

## Overview
Clouddash offers flexible upgrade and downgrade options to match your organization's needs. Changes take effect immediately with proration applied to your invoice.

## Upgrade Policy

### When You Can Upgrade
- Anytime during your billing cycle
- No waiting period required
- Changes effective immediately
- Prorated pricing applied

### Upgrade Process

1. **Go to Account Settings**
   - Navigate to **Settings** → **Billing** → **Plans**

2. **Select New Plan**
   - Click on desired plan (Professional, Enterprise)
   - Review features and pricing
   - Click **Upgrade to [Plan Name]**

3. **Billing Changes**
   - Current month: Difference prorated and added to next invoice
   - Example: Upgrade mid-month with $30 remaining
   - Annual subscription: Discount applied to new plan

4. **Confirm Upgrade**
   - Review new plan features
   - Verify payment method on file
   - Click **Confirm Upgrade**
   - Upgrade completes immediately

### What Happens During Upgrade

**Immediate Changes:**
- New features available immediately
- Increased team member limits applied
- Higher API rate limits enabled
- Enhanced support tier activated (if applicable)

**No Downtime:**
- Service continues uninterrupted
- Dashboards remain accessible
- Data retained in full
- Alert rules unaffected

### Prorated Charges

**Example Calculation:**
- Current plan: Starter ($50/month)
- Upgrade to: Professional ($100/month)
- Upgrade on day 15 of 30-day month
- Days remaining: 15
- Professional daily rate: $100 ÷ 30 = $3.33/day
- Prorated charge: $3.33 × 15 = $49.95
- Amount credited from Starter: $50 × 15/30 = $25
- Net charge to next invoice: $49.95 - $25 = $24.95

## Downgrade Policy

### Downgrade Restrictions

**Cannot Downgrade Mid-Cycle If:**
- Features used exceed new plan's limits
- Team members exceed new plan's maximum
- Custom integrations exceed new plan's limits

**Can Downgrade If:**
- New plan supports your current usage
- Team size within new limit
- Features used available on new plan

### Downgrade Process

1. **Review Current Usage**
   - Settings → Billing → Usage Dashboard
   - Check team members count
   - Verify features in use
   - Ensure new plan supports usage

2. **Request or Schedule Downgrade**
   - Click **Downgrade** from plans page
   - Select target plan
   - Choose effective date:
     - Immediate: Effective today
     - End of cycle: Effective after current billing period
   - Recommended: End of cycle to avoid complexity

3. **Confirmation**
   - Review downgrade summary
   - Understand feature changes
   - Confirm payment reduction
   - Submit downgrade request

4. **Downgrade Effective**
   - Takes effect on selected date
   - Credit applied to account
   - Confirmation email sent

### What Happens During Downgrade

**Immediate Changes:**
- Plan limits take effect
- Reduced team member slots (may need to remove users)
- API rate limits reduced
- Premium features may become unavailable

**Data Preservation:**
- Historical data retained
- Dashboards not deleted
- Alerts remain configured (but may be limited)
- Integration connections preserved

**If Features Exceed Limits:**
- Example: Downgrade from 25 to 5 team members
- Current 15 team members on system
- Action required: Remove 10 members
- Removed members lose access but can re-add within 30 days

### Downgrade Timing

**Best Practices:**
- Schedule downgrade for end of billing cycle
- Avoid downgrading with active projects
- Notify team of changes in advance
- Plan feature usage for new plan tier

**Downgrade Effective Dates:**
- Immediate: Changes today (prorated credit applied)
- End of Cycle: Changes when current period ends
- Future Date: Scheduled downgrade on specific date
- Cancellation: Downgrade to free tier (last option)

## Cancellation

### Cancellation Policy

- Cancel anytime with no penalty
- Effective end of current billing cycle
- Data exported before deletion
- 14-day grace period to restore

### Cancellation Process

1. Contact support or use self-service option
2. Confirm cancellation intent
3. Effective date confirmed (end of cycle)
4. Access gradually revoked
5. Data available for export during grace period

## Refund Policy

### Refunds Available For

- Unused portion of subscription
- Prorated based on days used
- Must request within 30 days of charge

### Refunds NOT Available For

- Plans downgraded to lower tier
- Month-to-month after setup
- Services already rendered
- Custom development or consulting

### Refund Request Process

1. Go to **Settings** → **Billing** → **Invoices**
2. Find relevant invoice
3. Click **Request Refund** or contact support
4. Specify reason
5. Submit request
6. Approval typically within 5 business days

## Family Plan Switching

### Switching Between Related Plans

- Starter → Professional: Full upgrade available
- Professional → Enterprise: Available, includes custom features
- Enterprise → Professional: Requires feature downgrade confirmation

### Not Included in Switching

- Perpetual licenses cannot be upgraded/downgraded
- Legacy plans have unique terms

## Billing Cycle Management

### Annual vs Monthly Billing

**Monthly:**
- Bill every 30 days
- More flexible
- Easier to upgrade/downgrade
- Can switch anytime

**Annual:**
- Discount: 15-20% off monthly rate
- Locks in rate for 12 months
- Commitment required
- Can change plan mid-year (prorated)

### Anniversary Date

- Date subscription renews each year
- Next renewal date shown on billing page
- Can request date change (special requests)
- Upgrade/downgrade changes anniversary calculation

## Special Situations

### Enterprise Custom Plans

For Enterprise tier customers:

- Dedicated support team handles changes
- May have custom upgrade/downgrade terms
- Volume discounts applied
- Contact Account Manager for modifications

### Non-Profit Organizations

- Non-profits may qualify for discount
- Special down-tier options available
- Contact sales@clouddash.io for details
- Discount may not combine with annual

### Free Trial to Paid

- Trial includes Starter features
- Upgrade to Professional during trial
- After trial, convert to paid plan
- Or cancel with no charges

## Best Practices

- Review usage monthly to optimize plan tier
- Schedule downgrades for end of cycle
- Keep payment method current
- Monitor team member limits
- Plan for seasonal usage changes
- Use annual plan for long-term commitment and savings
